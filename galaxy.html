Introduction


The Milky Way Galaxy is a barred spiral galaxy that is home to our solar system. It is one of billions of galaxies in the universe, and it has captivated the human imagination for centuries.

Structure

The Milky Way consists of a central bar-shaped region surrounded by spiral arms. It contains stars, planets, gas, and dust. The galaxy's center hosts a supermassive black hole known as Sagittarius A*.

Characteristics

Our galaxy is estimated to be around 100,000 to 120,000 light-years in diameter. It is part of the Local Group of galaxies, which includes the Andromeda Galaxy and others.

Exploration

Exploration of the Milky Way Galaxy has been carried out by astronomers and space agencies. Telescopes and spacecraft, such as the Hubble Space Telescope, have provided valuable data about our galaxy.
